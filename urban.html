Design objectives included renovation of a residential property designed by simp.L Landscape Architects, located in Portland, Oregon. Major challenges associated with existing grade, a double fronted lot, and an existing garage that had been built into the hillside made integrating the clients program needs very difficult. The solution was to create three distinct living spaces at different elevations. The lowest tier was the play area where the site had to be raised about 12 feet to match the top of the existing garage. The middle tier became an auxiliary patio space, and the upper tier was to become a deck dedicated to formal dining. Specimen, existing Japanese Maples were protected as well as open views of downtown Portland and the West Hills beyond.
