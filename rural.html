Design objectives included demolition and reconstruction of an existing, urban parking lot located at Fifth and Market streets in downtown Portland. The site offered major grading and structural challenges as well as a sensitive historical context. Site design by simp.L Landscape Architects and CES Northwest.
Design objectives included restoration of a residential estate designed by simp.L Landscape Architects, of Portland, Oregon. Following an intensive site walk through, a detailed evaluation report was written to identify all failures in landscape architectural design intent following three years of neglect and poor maintenance. The response was to mitigate said failures associated with all trades. These images show our results one year after restoration. Plantings in these images were selected and placed by Drew.
Design objectives included adding site amenities such as a sport court, access walks, dock/patio area and plantings. Challenges in achieving land use approval/permits for the dock portion of the project existed due to sensitive jurisdictional entities who regulate development along the Willamette River Greenway. Site design by Drew c/o All Oregon Landscaping, Inc.
